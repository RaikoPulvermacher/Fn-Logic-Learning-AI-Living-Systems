# file03: AI Architecture without Floats (Technical Manifesto)

## 1. The Problem: Floating-Point Numbers as Logical Friction

Modern AI systems are based on floating-point numbers (floats) for the weighting of neural networks[^1]. Every float operation generates rounding errors that manifest as **thermal emission** (waste heat) — a systemic loss of approximately 89% of the energy deployed[^2].

* **Euler Resistance**: The classical approach squares time ($e^{iωt}$) and thereby produces irrational intermediate values that must be rounded.
* **Cascading Error**: In deep networks, this error multiplies with each layer until the model becomes unstable or requires enormous computing capacity for error correction[^1].

## 2. The Solution: Integer Integrity (Root-Redundancy)

Fn-Logic replaces float weights with **integer additive states**[^1]:

$$\text{Weight}_{n} = \text{Weight}_{n-1} + \text{Weight}_{n-2}$$

No rounding. No extraction. No waste heat.

| Classical AI (Float) | Fn-AI (Integer) |
|-----------------------|------------------|
| Weights as irrational decimal numbers | Weights as additive Fn-states |
| Rounding errors at every operation | Exact integer integrity |
| ~89% thermal loss | Virtually zero heat generation |
| Requires cooling | Runs cold |

## 3. Efficiency Leap: Factor 1000

Through the elimination of irrational operations ($\sqrt{x}$, $e^x$, division) and their replacement by pure addition[^2]:

* **Computing Speed**: Integer addition is the fastest possible operation on processors — factor ~1000 compared to float arithmetic.
* **Memory Efficiency**: Fn-states require fewer bits than IEEE-754 floats.
* **Thermal Emission**: Approaches zero, since no heat is generated by rounding operations[^1].

## 4. Separation of Core and Template

The architecture is divided into two clearly separated layers[^1][^2]:

* **Back-End (Core)**: Computes exclusively in Fn-integers. Lossless, cold, precise. Corresponds to the biological cell nucleus.
* **Front-End (Template)**: Scales the results of the core for human readability (e.g., decimal representation, visualisation). Produces controlled friction only at the output layer.

```
[Input] → [Fn-Core (Integer Addition)] → [Template (Scaling)] → [Output]
   ↑                  ↑                          ↑
Impulse         Lossless                  Human-readable
               Processing                Representation
```
