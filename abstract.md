# Scientific Abstract

## Title

**Fn-Logic: A Process-Oriented Integer Learning System as a Biologically Inspired AI Architecture Model**

---

## Abstract

This paper introduces the **Fn-Logic Learning System** — a theoretical framework for lossless machine learning and AI architecture that translates biological process principles into a mathematical-technical model.

The starting point is the observation that classical computing systems are built on Euler-based floating-point arithmetic (IEEE 754 floats), generating systemic losses in the form of rounding errors and thermal emission. The documented efficiency of such systems is estimated at approximately 37% ($1/e$), corresponding to a loss cascade of nearly 89%.

As an alternative, the **additive process chain of the Fn-Sequence** ($f_{n} = f_{n-1} + f_{n-2}$) is proposed. It forms the mathematical foundation of a recursive, state-preserving learning model. Every state in the system contains the complete information of its history of creation; back-calculation via root extraction or division is structurally unnecessary (**Root-Redundancy**).

A central model of this paper is the **U-U-E Learning Cycle** (Odd–Odd–Even), which describes three universal phases of learning: Impulse, Growth, and Understanding (Symmetry-Saturation). This rhythm corresponds to the cellular process cycle of living systems, which — according to the Fn-Sequence — reaches its saturation point after exactly 11 steps at $F_{11} = 89$. From this the paper derives the **89:11 Energy Integrity Model**: 89% of energy remains as directed process amplitude, while 11% is expended to maintain system coherence.

On this theoretical basis, the **Fn-AI Architecture** (Dual-Layer Architecture) is described. It divides every computing unit into two strictly separated layers:

1. **Core (Back-End)**: Processing exclusively in atomic integers (femtometre/Planck-tick scale). No irrational operations, no rounding errors, no thermal emission.
2. **Mask (Front-End)**: Scaling of core numbers into human-readable output formats (e.g., decimal-point shift). One-way principle: the mask must never write back into the core.

This separation directly mirrors the design of biological cells: the cell nucleus (DNA replication, lossless) and the cell membrane (external communication, controlled friction) structurally correspond to the core and mask of the Fn-Architecture. As a theoretical efficiency estimate, a speed advantage of integer addition over float arithmetic of approximately factor 1000 is given, since integer addition is the fastest available operation on processor architectures.

This paper is intended as a theoretical concept and architecture paper. Empirical benchmarks and implementation results are the explicit subject of future research.

**Keywords**: Fn-Logic, Fibonacci sequence, lossless learning, integer arithmetic, biologically inspired AI, dual-layer architecture, anti-floats, U-U-E cycle, 89:11 saturation, thermal emission, cognitive process modelling
